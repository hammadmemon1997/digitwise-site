Medicare Levy

Most Australian residents pay a 2% Medicare levy on their taxable income. A reduced levy or full exemption applies if your income is below the low-income threshold ($26,000 for individuals in FY2024–25).

  • Full levy: 2% of taxable income above $26,000
  • Shade-in threshold: $23,226 to $26,000
  • Surcharge: extra 1–1.5% if no private hospital cover and income >$93,000
🎓

HELP / HECS Repayments

If you have a HELP debt, repayments are automatically deducted by your employer once your income reaches the compulsory repayment threshold of $54,435 in 2024–25.

  • Repayment rates scale from 1% to 10%
  • Based on repayment income, not taxable income
  • Debt is indexed to CPI annually on 1 June
🏦

Superannuation

Your employer must contribute 11.5% of your ordinary time earnings to super in FY2024–25. This amount is on top of your salary and is shown separately in this calculator.

  • SG rate: 11.5% (FY2024–25)
  • Rises to 12% from 1 July 2025
  • Annual concessional cap: $30,000
Frequently asked questions
How is income tax calculated in Australia?
Australian income tax is calculated using a progressive rate system. You pay 0% on the first $18,200, then 19% on income from $18,201 to $45,000, then 32.5% from $45,001 to $120,000, 37% from $120,001 to $180,000, and 45% on everything above $180,000. This calculator applies all these rates automatically, plus the Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) of up to $700.
What is the Low Income Tax Offset (LITO)?
The LITO reduces the amount of income tax you pay if you earn under $66,667. The maximum offset is $700 for incomes up to $37,500. It phases out at 5 cents per dollar between $37,500 and $45,000, and then at 1.5 cents per dollar between $45,000 and $66,667. This calculator automatically includes the LITO in all calculations.
Is super included in my salary or on top?
Super is on top of your salary. If your employer says you earn $80,000 salary, they also pay $9,200 in super contributions separately (11.5% x $80,000). Your take-home pay is calculated from your base salary only. This calculator shows super as a separate line so you can see your total package.
What is the effective tax rate vs the marginal rate?
Your effective tax rate is the total tax you pay as a percentage of your total income. Your marginal rate is the rate applied to the last dollar you earn. For example, on a $90,000 salary your marginal rate is 32.5%, but your effective rate might be around 21% because only part of your income is taxed at the higher rate.
Can I use this calculator for a second job?
If this is a second job, you generally should not claim the tax-free threshold. Select "No — not claiming" in the tax-free threshold dropdown. Your employer will withhold tax at the standard rate for your income level across both jobs.
